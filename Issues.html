Abortion


Abortion is a women's reproductive issue where the pregnancy of a human fetus is terminated prematurely before the women has brought the fetus to term or birth. In the landmark case, Roe v Wade the Supreme Court Of The United States declared that the right to privacy guranteed to people in the United States in the constitution was broad enough legally speaking to encompass a women's decision to carry out an abortion.

There are dozens of possible stances on abortion but they all fall generally into one of a few catagories. Someone who is pro-choice would believe strongly that abortion should be legally protected and that the decision should be left to the women. Conversely, a pro-life stance would be one that argues for the legal abolition of legally guranteed abortion. People's stances may also fall somewhere in between the two camps for instance theat abortion should only be allowed in instances of rape or when carrying the fetus to term could potentially threaten the mother's life. There are also those who are morally against abortion due to social or religious beliefs but also do not believe that the government has the right to regulate a female's body. In general, conservatives tend to be more pro-life while liberals tend to be more pro-choice, but as with every issue, the divide in opinion never breaks neatly down party lines and there is a large populaiton that deviate from this over simplified generilization.

Government Bailouts


Leading up to the year 2008, banks began making risky investments in sub-prime mortgages. Sub-prime mortgages were mortages given to people who otherwise would not have gotten a mortgage and when the housing bubble burst, people were left paying a mortgage on homes that were worth less than they had bought them for making paying for them frivilous. Theses mortages were then packaged and sold on the financial market as mortgage-backed securtities and in fact the ownership of these special securtities caused the Largest bankrupcy in U.S. history when Lehman Brothers filed for Chapter 11 Bankrupcy protection in September of 2008. Just two months earlier the Pasadena California based IndyMac mortgage corporation was seized by the federal government and placed into the recievership of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation where its assets were acquired by one west bank signaling the begining of the 2008 financial crisis and was the largest bank failure up until that of Lehman Brothers just a few months later. The failure of IndyMac was preceeded by U.S. senator Charles Schumer warning the public about IndyMac and resulted in a bank run with customers withdrawing more than 7.5% of the banks total on hand assets in just a few days. The failure of IndyMac then caused bank runs at other banks across the country, namely Washington Mutual where 9.4 billion dollars were withdrawn in the following days. After Moody's downgraded Washington Mutual's debt antoher bank run ensued and the bank too was seized by the federal government and placed in the Recievership of The Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation where its $307 billion in assets were sold off to J.P. Morgan Chase.

The Future of the U.S. economy seemed bleek so the secretary of the tresary with the cooperation of President George W. Bush drafted legislation that would see Washington aquire $700 Billion of these risky mortgage backed securities from financial institutions in hopes that no more large banks would fail detrimentally to the economy. Th idea was that the banks contolled individually so much of the domestic and global financial systems that even letting just one fail would destroy the economy possibly leading to a depression. The banks that recieved taxpayer money however in some cases had no obligation to pay back the government for the bailout money and bankers never faced any criminal charges for essentially bringing the largest economy in the world to a screeching hault. The stimulus package was then later extended by President Obama to include american Auto Makers General Motors and Chrysler. The Federal government obtained a 60% controlling stake in General Motors for just shy of $70 Billion and fired its then ceo and led the company towards a restructuring due to their strategic economic importance. The government also forced Chrysler to merge with Italian Fiat Motors to form Fiat Chrysler Group in order to ensure their survival.

The political debate rages on over weather the federal goverment has the right to appropriate taxpayer money to private corporations and if they do have this right, then to what extent should it be used. Should it only be used to bail out too big to fail banks? only small banks? Only firms strategically important to the economy?



Possible Stances

● The Government Has no right to appropriate taxpayer money to fund private industry

● Too-big to fail banks and companies should be allowed to fail and auctioned off to the highest bidder

● Too-big to fail banks and companies should be broken up before they have the chance to cause harm to the economy again

Minimum Wage


Since 2009 The federal Miniumum wage has been set at $7.25 per hour. Four States, Arkansas, Georgia, Minnesota and Wyoming however have state minimum wage requirements that are lower than that set by the federal government but these are not implemented in practice thanks to the supremecy clause of the constitution that states that if a conflicting law exists on a state and federal level than the federal law is always applied nullifying the state law. According to the Pew Research center, the minimum wage peaked in 1968 at about $8.68 acccounting for inflation and that in the almost 50 years since them minimum wage earners have lost nearly 10% of their purchasing power due to inflation. Some would simply like the Minimum wage to keep up with the rate of inflation rate while others want a Living Wage like that demended by the fight for $15 movement. A Living Wage is what some consider the minimum to live comfortably and get by and detractors of the Living Wage argument would point out that minimum wage positions are intended for younger employees and that they are entry-level positions and that workers should put in the effort for job advancement. Cities Like San Francisco and Seattle have worked towards a goal of a $15 Living Wage minimum. Detractors would like to point out that prices of goods and services under this law would rise in prices and that rise in prices would lead to faster rates of inflation. Another argument against a higher minimum wage is that it would eliminate jobs as companies would rather eliminate low skill jobs especially in service positions and replace them with robots and that these lay-offs would cause strain on the social welfare systems.



Possible Stances

● States Should Set the Minimum Wage, not the federal government

● The Federal Government can set the minimum but states have the power to raise it if they like

● The Federal Government Should mandate a living wage

● The Federal Government should ensure that the minimum wage is a set value that is adjusted continiously for inflation

● The Federal Government should keep the minimum wage at the same level as it is currently

● The Federal Government should set higher minimum wages in "development zones" or low-income neighborhoods where individuals are more likely to work in and thus depend on the invcome from a minimum-wage job

● The Federal Government should keep the minimum wage at the same level but provide social safety net assistance for those below a certain threshold that extends above the poverty line

Climate Change


Climate Change is the idea that there is a global change in climate patterns and that humans caused it. This is probably one of the more messy political debates but presented here are a variety of stances one could take. 10,883 of 10,885 peer reviewed scientific articles and journal entries agree that climate change is a real phonomenon and that at least to some extent caused it. Exxon mobil had knowledge of this phonomenon as early as the late 1970's but chose not to share this information with the general public. Most scientists predict a global increade in mean teparatures of around 1 degree centigrade and that along with the loss of the polar ice caps, global sea levels could rise as much as 20 feet. With the potential for global catastrophe, some argue that the science is out and that with past events like the ice age the global temparatures too changed drastically and that humans are not the cause of the current temparature fluctuations. Others want the government to take a harline stance in providing funding for alternative energy sources like solar and wind to avoid the potential climate calamity.



Possible Stances

Homosexuals in the Military


For a long time the military has been a place of discrimination against homosexual service members. Prior to 1993 homosexuals were barred from service all together but this ban was lifted in that year with the policy of don't ask don't tell. The basic premise of this policy was that a servicemember would not be asked about their orientation and they would not be discharged simply for being gay but that if they had any relations while in the army or told anyone that would be considered homosexual activity and as such the service member would then be discharged. Don't ask don't tell was repealed in 2010 and since 2011 service members have the right to serve without worring about being discharged for their orientation.

Transgender Bathroom Rights


In 2016 the state of North Carolina passed it's so called bathroom law and followed it up with another law stating that local municipalities could not pass their own law annuling the bathroom law in their own jurisdictions. The bathroom law said that transgender people must use the bathroom on their birth certificate rather then the gender they identify as. The rationality as supporters note is that it keeps people from pretending to be a gender just to access the bathroom reserved for another gender. Opponents argue that it legalizes discrimination and that the justification is creating a straw man for which people can justifiably attack rather than the reality which is that these people are just going about their normal daily lives



Possible Stances

● They should use the bathroom on their birth certificate

● They should use the bathroom they identify as

● Public places should have a third option i.e. the family restroom in many establishments
